From here the setting sun had only begun to soak the town below in its warm orange hue, and although beautiful, time is running out.


I steady my breath, ready to pull back my favoured…

Weapon Choice: Bow & Arrow

+ Bow & Arrow


[SFX: Arrow sliding against wood]


…bow and arrow.


My hands tighten across the grip. The bow was the only weapon I ever found myself proficient in.


I remember the mantra I had been telling myself earlier.


Keep my feet shoulder-width apart. Keep my aim true and steady. My shoulders lowered, not too tense.


[SFX: Arrow woosh and hit]


I take a stance and release.


It misses.


[SFX: Drawing bamboo from a quiver]


In the same beat, I reach for my side-belt quiver and draw the next arrow. I convince myself that I am pushing out a fog from my mind’s eye. That I only need practice.


[SFX: Arrow woosh and dull thud]


The arrow falters again.


And again.


At one point, when I held a bow, I felt most like myself, but now…


My aim’s only gotten worse. My draw is weak.

Weapon Choice: Sword

+ Sword


[SFX: Sword unsheathing]

…sword.


My hands wrap around the hilt. The two-sided blade has always been an extension of myself. It was the only thing I knew best.


[SFX: Sword swoosh]


It’s ingrained now that my left foot steps forward, my right foot back, my knees bent. I repeat the mantra as I swivel to strike a non-existent enemy.


It feels weak.


I go again, this time my thumb positioned ever so slightly under the gilded hilt, I convince myself that I’m pushing out the fog from my mind’s eye. That I only need to practice.


At some point, when I held my sword, I felt most like myself, but now…


[SFX Sword Swoosh]

[SFX: Sword dull clatter?]


I shift my weight, guiding the blade with my hand. In my final swing a dull sensation ripples through my arm, and the sword falls.


My form is stiff. I keep throwing my weight around needlessly.


—> Talk to Maya

Weapon Choice: Fist

+ Fist


[SFX: Metal clang?]


… knuckle-dusters.


My thumbs trail against the grain and spikes. They were the truest extension of myself. Light, but strong enough to do irreversible damage.


The last time I entered a fight, I was nearly laughed out, but people seldom know what to do in wild close combat. I thrived on knowing it was my own power.


[SFX: Air punches? General woosh noise?]


I took my stance, throwing jabs and feigning dodges against an unseen enemy. Left foot forward, right heel back and raised, knees slightly bent for a strong centre of gravity.


[SFX: Punches]


One, two, one, two.


My fists clench between the carved wood, allowing me to focus and convince myself I was pushing the fog from my mind’s eye. That I simply need practice.


At some point, when I slipped these on, I felt most likely myself, but now…


I exhale. My chest tightens as I manoeuvre through the space in front.


My footwork and stamina have gotten sloppy.


—> Talk to Maya

Talk to Maya


My own hands don’t feel like they’re mine.


I try again to focus when a sudden, familiar voice calls out my name:


[INPUT: Player’s name. Default = Adel]



VOICE: MC!


[SFX: Running, soft ground?]

I pivot back, catching the owner of the voice sprinting up the hillside. She waves at me wildly, balancing the bounce in her gait with the bobbing satchel at her hip.

[SPRITE: Maya, shock? — Further back]

My best friend, Maya.

[SPRITE: Maya, concerned or shocked, closer in]

I rest my stance and meet her a pitiful half way. Her dark curls drop in front of me when she catches up.

MAYA: MC! I— You— Argh!

MAYA: Fire. My throat feels like it’s on fire!

MC: Maya, did you run up the entire hillside?

MC: How did you know I was here?


[SPRITE: Maya, eyes closed]

She waves her hand dismissively, trying to catch her breath.

MAYA: Why do— you always— hide when you train?


[SFX: Water pouring]

Maya gasps, reaching out with grabbing hands for my leather-bound flask. I hand it over and once she’s emptied every drop, she returns to her usual rejuvenated spark.

[SPRITE: Maya, eyes open]

MC: I wasn’t hiding. This is just a good spot to practice.

[SPRITE: Maya serious or angry]

MAYA: I looked everywhere for you. If you had just told me you were going to train, I could have been helping with your form!

She folds her arms assertively.

Whenever Maya took this stance, I knew I’d offended her in some way. Which was fine, because Maya was always offended.

[SPRITE: Maya relaxed, or neutral]

MAYA: You don’t have to be shy.

MAYA: I don’t mind helping you train.

I raise a brow.

MC: But you’re not here to train with me, are you?


[SPRITE: Maya, Grin/happy]

MAYA: Of course not! You clearly need me for something greater.

As always, there’s a worrying amount of determination in her voice.
